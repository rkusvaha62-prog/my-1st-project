Use Cases & Impact Stories

Behind every sensor, drone and mobile app, there is a real person whose life becomes a little easier and a little more secure. These stories show how technology quietly supports farmers and officers every day.

Farmer Story 1: Small Land + IoT Sensors + Mobile App

Water Saving • Small Farmer

A farmer with just 2 acres of land installs IoT soil moisture sensors in two plots. The mobile app starts telling him when each plot really needs water. He slowly stops irrigating “by habit” and begins to trust the data on his screen.

Instead of guessing, he irrigates only when required. Over the season, he notices that the motor runs less, the field never stays dry for long, and his crop looks healthier. The result is almost 30% water saving and a better yield, because the crop never faces long dry stress.

Impact: 30% less water, more peace of mind.

Farmer Story 2: Drone for Early Disease Detection

Crop Health • Drone View

During the main growth stage, a drone flies over a group of fields and quietly captures dozens of images. On the ground everything looks normal, but in the processed drone map, a corner of one farm shows faded yellow patches.

It is an early sign of nutrient deficiency and pest attack. The farmer and extension worker visit only that corner, confirm the issue and treat the affected spot. Instead of spraying the entire field, they treat only where it is needed, reducing pesticide cost and protecting the rest of the crop.

Impact: Less pesticide, problem caught before it spreads.

Farmer Story 3: Automatic Irrigation Based on Moisture

Smart Pump • Time Saved

A vegetable grower connects his pump to a smart controller linked with soil moisture sensors. Whenever the moisture drops below the threshold, the controller quietly turns the pump ON. When the soil reaches the target range, it turns the pump OFF.

Earlier, he had to visit the field late at night or early morning just to check the water. Sometimes he forgot, and over-irrigation caused root diseases. Now, irrigation matches the crop’s real need. He saves time, electricity and avoids the guilt of wasting water.

Impact: Healthy roots, less tension about “did I switch the pump off?”.

Government Officer: Dashboard for Better Planning

District View • Policy Impact

At the district level, agriculture officers open a dashboard that combines sensor data, drone images and satellite-based indices. Instead of reading long reports, they see drought-prone villages, pest outbreak zones and low-productivity clusters on a single screen.

With this clarity, they can plan where to send field staff, which farmers need training first, and where to focus input support. Schemes feel less like “paper work” and more like targeted help reaching the right farmers at the right time.

Impact: Smarter decisions, resources used where they matter most.
