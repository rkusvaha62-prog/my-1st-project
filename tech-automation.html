Automation & Smart Irrigation Devices

Automation devices use data from IoT sensors to control pumps, valves and sprinklers so that water is applied at the right time and in the right quantity.

1. What is Smart Irrigation Automation?

Smart irrigation uses controllers and actuators (relays, valves) to switch irrigation systems ON or OFF automatically based on soil moisture, time schedule or weather forecast.

2. How does it work in Agriculture?

  • IoT sensors send soil moisture data to the controller or cloud.
  • Predefined rules (e.g., “if moisture < 30% then start pump”) are checked.
  • Controller activates pump or opens valves to irrigate the field.
  • When moisture reaches target value, system stops irrigation automatically.
  • Farmer can also override settings using the mobile app.

3. Why are we using Automation? (Advantages)

  • Prevents under-irrigation and over-irrigation.
  • Saves water and electricity.
  • Reduces labour and time spent in manually operating pumps.
  • Ensures irrigation even when farmer is away from farm.

4. Example Use Cases

  • Night-time irrigation automatically controlled by timer and moisture sensor.
  • Different irrigation cycles for different crop zones in the same farm.
  • Remote control of pump using mobile app (ON/OFF, schedule settings).

5. Limitations / Challenges

  • Initial installation cost and wiring / plumbing requirements.
  • Need for reliable power supply and protection from lightning / surges.
  • Farmer training required to manage settings and maintenance.
