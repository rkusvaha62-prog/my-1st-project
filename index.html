Precision Agriculture using Modern Technologies

Traditional farming is mostly area-based: same crop, same irrigation, same fertilizer plan for the whole village. Our approach is data-based: every field is monitored separately using IoT sensors, drones, satellites and a mobile app so that farmers get field-specific recommendations.

This system continuously watches soil, weather and crop health and converts raw data into simple messages for farmers like “Irrigate today evening” or “Spray only in the north side plot”.

Key Modern Technologies

Each technology below opens in a separate page with full information on what it is, how it works in agriculture and why we use it. On this home page we show a quick summary and a small explanation on the side.

IoT Sensors

Measure soil moisture, temperature, pH and nutrient levels directly in the field.

  • Installed at different points inside the farm.
  • Send readings automatically every few minutes.
  • Work on battery or small solar panels.
  • Connect to gateway / mobile through Wi-Fi, LoRa, or GSM.
Open full information →
How it helps farmers:

With IoT sensors we don’t have to guess whether the soil is dry or wet. The system continuously checks moisture, pH and NPK and shows a simple color status: green = OK, yellow = caution, red = problem. Farmers can see this on their mobile app, know exactly when to irrigate and avoid over-watering and over-fertilizing the land.

Drones

Capture aerial images to detect crop stress, pests and waterlogging early.

  • Fly above the field and take HD or thermal images.
  • Cover large area in very short time.
  • Detect patch-wise stress not visible from ground.
  • Can also be used for spraying in targeted zones.
Open full information →
Why drones are important:

Human eye can see only from the edge of the plot. Drone images give a top view of the entire farm and analytics highlight areas where plants are weak or attacked by pests. This allows spot treatment instead of spraying chemicals everywhere, which saves money and also protects the environment.

Satellites

Provide large-scale data on weather, rainfall and vegetation index for planning.

  • Give NDVI / vegetation index for crop health trend.
  • Help in monitoring big areas: village, block, district.
  • Useful for drought / flood monitoring and planning.
  • Supports government schemes and crop insurance.
Open full information →
Planning at bigger scale:

Satellites are like the “eyes in the sky” for agriculture departments and FPOs. They can see overall crop health, sowing area and growth stage. Combining satellite data with field sensors gives a full picture – micro level and macro level – which is useful for advance warnings and better policy decisions.

Farmer Mobile App

Delivers advisories, alerts and recommendations in simple local language.

  • Shows live data from sensors, drones and satellites.
  • Gives voice + text messages for low-literacy users.
  • Sends alerts for irrigation, fertilizer and pest control.
  • Stores field history and previous crop performance.
Open full information →
Bridge between technology and farmer:

All complex analytics finally come to the farmer through a simple mobile screen. The app converts technical terms into easy “Do / Don’t do” type suggestions, supports the local language and can also work in offline mode with SMS/IVR for remote areas.

Cloud & AI Analytics

Stores and analyzes data to generate smart, field-specific decisions.

  • Collects data from sensors, drones, satellites and weather APIs.
  • Uses AI/ML models to predict irrigation, yield and disease risk.
  • Creates farmer-wise and plot-wise digital records.
  • Helps in long-term planning and benchmarking.
Open full information →
Brain of the entire system:

Cloud servers work like a central brain. They receive raw readings and images, clean them and run AI algorithms to generate advice. Over time the system learns from previous seasons and becomes more accurate, so that every farmer gets personalised recommendations based on his field history.

Automation & Smart Irrigation

Automatically controls pumps and valves based on real-time soil moisture.

  • Turns motor ON/OFF based on sensor threshold values.
  • Supports drip, sprinkler and traditional canal systems.
  • Can be operated remotely from mobile app.
  • Reduces labour and prevents over-irrigation at night.
Open full information →
From advice to action:

Instead of only telling the farmer “irrigate now”, smart irrigation can actually start and stop the pump automatically. This saves time, avoids night visits to the field and ensures that every crop row receives the right amount of water at the right time, improving yield and saving electricity.

Overall Impact

  • Increase crop yield and quality using data-driven decisions.
  • Reduce wastage of water, fertilizers and pesticides.
  • Improve soil health and protect the environment.
  • Support government schemes through accurate, real-time data.
  • Create digital records for each farmer and each plot for future planning.
